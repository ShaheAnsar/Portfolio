Heated Etchant Bath (Embedded Systems)

On-off temperature controller for a chemical etchant bath. Wanted to see how I'd fare with a safety critical design - you do not want to overheat the etchant. I used a heater and a relay to heat the waterbath. The etchant is kept in the water bath. The delta is carefully monitored and switched off if it gets close to a set limit. One hiccup - the display I was using had a weird non-standard version of I2C that the Atmega I used does not support, so I had to bit-bang the "I2C" display. I hate I2C with a passion but the bit-banging was fun. View project

Guitar Amplifier (Signal Processing, Power Amplification)

Opamp-based front-end with effects and TDA2030 power amplifier. Implements distortion and 3-band EQ. My cheap amplifier broke, so I designed a simple one instead using parts I had on hand. Lesson - always have a bunch of audio components on hand.

3 Stage Planetary Gearbox for a NEMA17

As part of building small robots, I wanted something that would be simple, cheap and most importantly for me - something I can build when the inspiration hits. So I built this. It was incredibly fun since I had only designed simple gear trains before this. It was my first go at a proper gearbox. I wanted strength and repairability to be a highlight (Yes, repairability, because plastic gears). All rotating components do so on small ball bearings. To improve the strength of the gears, I decided to put screws in them - an increase in weight in exchanges for higher strength. The failures shifted from sheared layers to sheared teeth with a higher loading capacity. The stages can be stacked one on top of each other to increase the reduction, but the current limitation is the way the sun gear is coupled to the shaft - it slips under higher reduction ratios. The CAD file is not very well organized, so please go easy one me lol View project
